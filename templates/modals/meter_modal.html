I live in a co-op (a big house with 30 other students), and each semester we have an event called Room to Room, where everyone decorates their room with a theme. Last fall I decided to do a Scientology themed room. I wore aviator glasses and pretended to be Tom Cruz.

I built a homemade "E-Meter" that measured your conductance. I connected wires to two metal handles then used an Arduino to send 5v across it. You would grab a handle with each hand and the Arduino measured the voltage differential. A servo was connected to the meter, and once the Arduino sensed a voltage increase, the servo swung to the right showing a high "Thetan" level (or whatever E-Meters are supposed to measure).

The Arduino was connected to my laptop via a serial connection. When it read a voltage increase, it activated a warning phrase, something like "warning, electrodermal activity at critical level". Then it used the plotly api to graph the voltage online.

My room had pretty mixed reactions. Some people thought it was the nerdiest thing they've ever seen, while some people thought it was like magic. People had a lot of fun figuring out how it worked, and my room was voted best in the house.

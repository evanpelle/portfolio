I live in a co-op (a big house with 30 other students), and each semester we have an event called Room to Room, where everyone decorates their room with a theme. Last fall I decided to do a Scientology themed room. I wore aviator glasses and pretended to be Tom Cruz.

I built a homemade "E-Meter" that measures your conductance. I wrapped two beer cans in aluminum foil, and then used an Arduino to send 5v across it. You grab a can in each hand and the Arduino measures the voltage differential. A servo is connected to the meter, and once the Arduino senses the voltage, the servo swings to the right showing a high "Thetan" level (or whatever E-Meters are supposed to read).

The Arduino is connected to my laptop via a serial connection. The laptop reads the voltage and when it notices a voltage, it activates a warning phrase, something like "warning, electrodermal activity at critical level". Then it uses the plotly api to graph the voltage online.

My room had pretty mixed reactions. Some people thought it was the nerdiest thing they've ever seem, while some people thought it was like magic. People had a lot of fun figuring out how it worked, and my room was voted best in the house.
